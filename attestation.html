Apostille vs Embassy Attestation

Understanding the difference between two major authentication methods

Apostille

Apostille Certificate

Apostille is a form of authentication applicable to countries that are members of the Hague Convention of 1961. It simplifies the process by requiring just one certification.

Applicable for:
  • Documents to be used in 118+ Hague Convention countries
  • Faster and simpler authentication process
  • Issued by the Ministry of External Affairs, India
Embassy

Embassy Attestation

Embassy attestation is required for countries that are not part of the Hague Convention. This process involves multiple levels of authentication, including verification by the embassy of the destination country.

Applicable for:
  • Documents to be used in Gulf countries (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, etc.)
  • Multi-stage verification process
  • Final authentication by the destination country's embassy
